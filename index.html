Revenue Forecasting

From ±22% Error to ±6%: Rebuilding the ARR Forecast

NovaTech Solutions was scaling fast but forecasting blind. Monthly board updates were built from gut-feel pipeline estimates, with no consistent methodology between AEs. Leadership couldn't reliably plan headcount or cash deployment.

I designed a tiered probability model in SQL — pulling stage age, deal size, and historical win rates by segment — then layered in a 3-month trailing average to smooth seasonal volatility. Outputs were surfaced in a Tableau dashboard updated daily.

  • Enterprise segment carried 3× the win-rate variance of SMB — isolated and modelled separately
  • Q3 pipeline had systematic over-inflation by 28% due to stale opportunities not being disqualified
  • Introduced a 90-day decay rule: deals with no activity downweighted automatically
  • Final model reduced board forecast error from ±22% → ±6% over 3 quarters

Cost Reduction

$1.3M in Savings Found in Plain Sight

Clearwave Logistics had grown from 40 to 180 employees in 18 months. Vendor contracts were signed ad-hoc with no central oversight. Five separate tools were doing the work of two. Billing ran on manual spreadsheets.

I ran a full operational cost audit — mapping all vendor spend, headcount cost per function, and process inefficiencies. I used SQL to extract 24 months of invoice data and categorized spend against benchmarks from comparable-stage companies.

  • SaaS tool sprawl: 11 overlapping tools consolidated to 6, saving $340K annually
  • Billing automation replaced 14 hrs/week of manual AR work — reallocated headcount
  • Vendor renegotiation on 3 contracts yielded 22% average price reduction
  • Recruiting cost-per-hire reduced 28% by restructuring agency vs. direct sourcing mix

GTM Analysis

ICP Segmentation That Actually Converted

Axon Retail Tech's sales team was chasing a broad market with no clear prioritization framework. Reps spent equal time on $5K and $500K opportunities. Win rates were flat. Marketing was generating volume but not quality.

I analyzed 18 months of closed-won and closed-lost data across firmographic dimensions — company size, vertical, tech stack, and deal cycle length. I enriched the dataset via Clay and built a scoring model that ranked inbound leads in real time.

  • Mid-market retail (100–500 employees) had 2.4× higher win rate vs. enterprise — designated primary ICP
  • Deals with a technical champion closed 38% faster and at 15% higher ACV
  • Removed bottom 30% of pipeline that consumed 40% of rep time with near-zero conversion
  • Lead-to-opportunity conversion improved 12% within 2 quarters of new segmentation
